General Info

Fyodor Mikhailovich Dostoyevsky was a philosophical novelist, short story writer, essayist, and journalist from Moscow, Russia. His date of birth was on the 11th of November, 1821, who passed away on the 9th of February, 1881, in St. Petersburg, Russia. Literary critics regard Dostoyevsky as one of the greatest novelists considered of all of world literature. Dostoyevsky's literary works explore the human condition in the troubled political, social, and spiritual atmospheres of 19th-century Russia, and engage with a variety of philosophical and religious themes. He has written over 20 and more works where his most known ones are his novels The Brothers Karamazov (1880), Demons (1872), The Idiot (1869), Crime and Punishment (1866), and his novella Notes from Underground (1864).
